“Our research team of evolutionary biologists conducted an extensive and thorough examination of human physiology, past and present, and determined that human beings were, in their ideal state, supposed to be born, spend a solid 12 hours awake as an infant, and then lie down for a tranquil, dream-filled sleep from which they would then not awaken,”lead researcher Dennis Zeveloff said of the findings, which also suggest that life for early man was not supposed to last longer than one day.
